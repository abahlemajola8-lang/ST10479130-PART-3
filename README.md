Name: Siphilisiwe Foundation of South Africa 
Organisation Description: 
This non-profit, community-based organization is dedicated to raising community knowledge of health issues and fighting gender-based violence (GBV). Through education, support services, and advocacy campaigns that tackle the causes and consequences of GBV, it seeks to empower young people and community members. Additionally, the organization offers HIV/AIDS awareness and prevention education, giving people the information and abilities they need to safeguard themselves and make wise health decisions. The organization works to make the community safer, healthier, and better informed through cooperation, outreach, and capacity-building initiatives.
Reasons for Choosing the Organisation: 
Many people are either directly impacted by or observe the effects of HIV/AIDS and gender-based violence (GBV) in their communities. A strong desire to change these problems might be sparked by witnessing or experiencing the social, emotional, and bodily pain they inflict. Someone may be inspired to start an organization that offers awareness, direction, and safe spaces for people to seek help in areas where access to education, support services, and candid conversations about these subjects is restricted. The objective is frequently to end the cycle of violence, lessen the stigma associated with HIV/AIDS, and provide community members and young people with the information and skills they need to protect themselves and create a safer future.
 Website Goals and Objectives:
Website Goals
•	 Raise community knowledge of GBV and HIV/AIDS by teaching people about the risks, preventative strategies, and support networks that are available.
•	Offer helpful information and resources, such as articles, manuals, help centre contact information, and self-defence advice.
•	Create content especially for young people, such as blogs, interactive discussions, and workshops, to empower and engage young people.
•	Promote community involvement in programs and campaigns, collaborations, and volunteerism to increase support and participation.
•	 Encourage reporting and assistance access to make it simpler for victims or impacted people to get support, report incidents, or seek counselling.

Get Involved
Donations

At the Siphilisiwe Foundation of South Africa, donations are investments in hope, dignity, and constructive change, not just monetary contributions. We are able to reach a wider audience and offer vital assistance to needy people and families throughout South Africa because to the kindness of people, companies, and community partners.

With each donation, we are able to maintain and improve our programs that protect vulnerable children, empower communities, promote emotional wellbeing, and address the horrific effects of gender-based violence. We are able to establish safe spaces, offer educational opportunities, provide psychosocial support services, and give people the tools they need to achieve better futures thanks to your help.

Donations are especially appreciated for our program for vulnerable children and orphans. Lack of essential school supplies is a major obstacle to schooling for many kids. Children can attend school with confidence and dignity thanks to donations of school uniforms, shoes, stationery, textbooks, school bags, sanitary products, and other educational supplies. In addition to promoting academic achievement, these efforts aid youngsters who frequently face challenging situations in regaining their sense of self-worth and belonging.

We appreciate donations of food boxes, clothing, toiletries, blankets, home goods, and other necessities that can support vulnerable families and people in addition to educational materials. These donations assist our larger goal of community development and empowerment while offering immediate aid.

No donation is insufficient. Every donation enables us to serve more people, address new needs in the community, and bring about long-lasting, sustainable change.

Monetary Donations

One of the best ways to help the Siphilisiwe Foundation of South Africa in its activities is through financial contributions. Donations of money provide us the freedom to react swiftly to pressing community issues and guarantee that our programs run smoothly all year long.

Your gift directly supports programs that address social issues and elevate marginalized communities, whether you decide to give a one-time donation or become a consistent monthly contributor. Program implementation, educational support, community outreach, counseling and emotional health services, advocacy campaigns against gender-based violence, skill development programs, and other community empowerment projects are all made possible by the funds received.

Donating allows you to become a part of a network of caring people and organizations that are dedicated to achieving social justice, gender equality, safer communities, and the protection of vulnerable children. Your sponsorship enables us to speak up for those whose voices are frequently ignored and offers workable answers to some of the problems that our communities face.

Giving on a regular basis has a significant impact because it enables us to develop and maintain long-term initiatives that produce significant and quantifiable results. Continuous funding enhances our ability to reach more beneficiaries, guarantees service continuity, and adds to our organization's overall sustainability.

Other Ways to Support

There are many ways to contribute beyond monetary donations. Individuals, businesses, schools, faith-based organizations, and community groups can support our mission by:
•	Donating school uniforms, stationery, and educational materials.
•	Providing food parcels and basic household necessities.
•	Sponsoring community outreach programmes and awareness campaigns.
•	Supporting fundraising events and initiatives.
•	Partnering with us through corporate social responsibility programmes.
•	Volunteering skills, expertise, or time to support our activities.
•	Helping raise awareness about our work within your networks and communities.

Make a Difference Today

Each donation is a chance to change people's lives. Through your gift, families can get vital support, communities can be empowered to create a brighter future, children can continue attending school, and survivors of gender-based violence can access support services.

By working together, we can bring about long-lasting change, give hope where it is most needed, and create communities that are safer, more resilient, and stronger for future generations. Your contribution can significantly improve the lives of individuals we serve, and your support is important.

Added Java-script

Explanation of my companies java-script code:
By including dynamic elements like a responsive navigation header, contact form validation, scroll animations, and page-load effects, this JavaScript code aims to enhance a website's user experience.

1. Header Show/Hide on Scroll
As the user scrolls down the page, the first part of the code manages the header of the website. The script searches for a <header> element after the page loads. If there is one, it keeps track of the user's scrolling behavior.

The header is hidden when the user scrolls down more than 80 pixels by adding the CSS class hidden. This expands the area available for content viewing. The hidden class is eliminated and the header is once again visible when the user scrolls back up. Every time the user scrolls more than 10 pixels, the code additionally adds a scrolled class to the <body> element. This class can be used to alter the page's appearance using CSS, for as adding a shadow to the header. 

2. Contact Form Validation and Submission
The contact form on the website is managed under the second section. The script starts by determining whether the page has a form and whether it has a submit button.
The script does basic validation and stops the browser's usual submission procedure when the user submits the form. It verifies that every field designated as necessary is filled up. An error message appears and the submission is halted if any required fields are left blank.

In order to stop duplicate submissions, the submit button is disabled and changes to "Sending..." if all mandatory fields are filled out.

Next, the script examines the action attribute of the form:
•	automatically generates an email with the data entered in the form and tries to open the user's default email program if the action is a mailto link.
•	The script uses the Fetch API to transfer the form data to a server if the action is an HTTP or HTTPS URL.
•	If the submission is successful, a "Sent" notification appears.
•	If a problem arises, the submit button is enabled again and an error message is displayed.

Additionally, the form has an aria-live accessible status section that enables screen readers to notify visually impaired users of submission outcomes.

3. Reveal-on-Scroll Animations
As the user scrolls down the website, animations are created in the third area to display content.

Sections, headings, detail blocks, and graphics are just a few of the components that the script chooses. These elements are first given a reveal class, which is usually applied in CSS to make them partially transparent or hidden.

Each element is monitored by the script using the IntersectionObserver API. An in-view class is added when at least 15% of an element is visible inside the browser window. This typically initiates a CSS animation, like a slide-in or fade-in effect. The observer quits keeping an eye on an aspect to enhance performance once it has been made public.

4. Page-Load Animation
The animations that happen when the page loaded are handled in the last section.

The script waits for the page to complete rendering and loading. Next, it gives the <body> element a page-ready class. When the page is ready, this class can be used in CSS to initiate visual effects like fading the page into view or smoothly presenting content.

Overall Purpose
This script's goal is to make the website more contemporary, interactive, and user-friendly. It:

•	Depending on the direction of the scroll, the navigation header is shown or hidden.
•	verifies the information on the form before submitting it.
•	accepts forms by email or a web server.
•	shows user feedback messages.
•	animates content as it moves into the viewport.
•	adds seamless page-load effects.
•	boosts performance and accessibility.

When combined, these elements give visitors a more polished and interesting browsing experience.
